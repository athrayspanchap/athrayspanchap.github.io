About Me

My name is Subramania Athiray Panchapakesan (I go by Athiray). I’m currently a NASA Postdoctoral Program Fellow at NASA Marshall Space Flight Center, Huntsville, AL, where I’m developing the Marshall Grazing Incidence X-ray Spectrometer (MaGIXS), sounding rocket experiment and study the solar X-ray emission to understand the heating time scales in the solar corona. Previously, I was a postdoctoral associate (2016 - 2019) at the University of Minnesota, Twin Cities, worked on the third rocket flight of Focusing Optics X-ray Solar Imager (FOXSI). I played an important role in the calibration of hard X-ray detectors FOXSI and studied the differential emission measure of small microflares by combining data from SDO/AIA, Hinode/XRT and FOXSI-2. In May of 2015, I completed my PhD in the Indian Space Resarch organisation, Indian Institute of Astrophysics under the supervison of Dr. Sreekumar. My dissertation, Study of lunar surface chemistry using Swept Charge Devices, can be found here.
